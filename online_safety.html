Online Safety and Cyber Awareness

Safety Measures While Being Online

Staying safe online requires vigilance and proactive measures. Here are some tips:

  • Use strong and unique passwords for your accounts.
  • Enable two-factor authentication whenever possible.
  • Avoid clicking on suspicious links or downloading unknown files.
  • Keep your software and antivirus updated.
  • Be cautious while sharing personal information on social media.

Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying involves using technology to harass, threaten, or humiliate others. Here's how to tackle it:

  • Don't respond to or engage with bullies online.
  • Block and report abusive users on social media platforms.
  • Keep evidence (screenshots, messages) if the situation escalates.
  • Reach out to trusted adults, counselors, or authorities if you're a victim.
  • Spread awareness and support anti-cyberbullying initiatives.

Intellectual Property Rights

Intellectual Property (IP) refers to creations of the mind, such as inventions, designs, and works of art, that are protected by law. To respect and safeguard IP online:

  • Always credit the original creator when sharing their work.
  • Avoid using copyrighted material without permission.
  • Understand the terms of Creative Commons licenses.
  • Register your own creations to protect them legally.
  • Educate yourself on copyright, trademarks, and patents.
